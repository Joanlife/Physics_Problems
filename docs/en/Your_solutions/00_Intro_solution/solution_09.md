# Task 09 – Optimization Problem

## Problem Statement

A rectangle lies under the curve

$y = 3 - x^2$

in the first quadrant.

Determine the rectangle with maximum area.

---

## Theory

The area of the rectangle equals width multiplied by height.  
The height is determined by the function value.

---

## Step-by-Step Solution

Area function:

$$
A = x(3 - x^2)
$$

$$
A = 3x - x^3
$$

Derivative:

$$
A' = 3 - 3x^2
$$

Set derivative to zero:

$$
3 - 3x^2 = 0
$$

$$
x^2 = 1
$$

$$
x = 1
$$

Height:

$$
y = 3 - 1
$$

$$
y = 2
$$

---

## Final Result

Rectangle dimensions:

$$
x = 1
$$

$$
y = 2
$$

Maximum area:

$$
A = 2
$$

---

## Interpretation

The rectangle with optimal dimensions balances width and height to maximize the area beneath the curve.

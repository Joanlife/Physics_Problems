# Task 02 – Systems of Equations

## Problem Statement

Solve the system of equations:

$2x + 3y = 12$

$x - y = 1$

---

## Theory

Systems of linear equations represent lines in the plane.  
Their solution corresponds to the intersection point of those lines.

The substitution method solves one equation for a variable and substitutes it into the other equation.

---

## Step-by-Step Solution

From the second equation:

$$
x - y = 1
$$

Solve for $x$:

$$
x = 1 + y
$$

Substitute into the first equation:

$$
2(1 + y) + 3y = 12
$$

$$
2 + 2y + 3y = 12
$$

$$
5y + 2 = 12
$$

$$
5y = 10
$$

$$
y = 2
$$

Substitute into the expression for $x$:

$$
x = 1 + y
$$

$$
x = 3
$$

---

## Final Result

$$
x = 3
$$

$$
y = 2
$$

---

## Interpretation

The ordered pair $(3,2)$ represents the unique solution that satisfies both equations simultaneously.

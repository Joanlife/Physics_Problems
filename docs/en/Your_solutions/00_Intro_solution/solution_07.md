# Task 07 – Logic & Series

## Problem Statement

A bicycle is 10 m from a wall and moves toward it at $1\,\text{m/s}$.

A fly moves between the bicycle and the wall at $2\,\text{m/s}$ until the bicycle reaches the wall.

Determine the total distance traveled by the fly.

---

## Theory

The key idea is to determine the total time until the bicycle reaches the wall.

The fly moves continuously during that time.

---

## Step-by-Step Solution

Distance to the wall:

$$
d = 10
$$

Bicycle speed:

$$
v = 1
$$

Time to reach the wall:

$$
t = \frac{d}{v}
$$

$$
t = 10
$$

Fly speed:

$$
v_f = 2
$$

Distance traveled by the fly:

$$
d_f = v_f t
$$

$$
d_f = 2 \times 10
$$

$$
d_f = 20
$$

---

## Final Result

$$
d_f = 20\ \text{m}
$$

---

## Interpretation

Although the fly changes direction infinitely many times, the total travel time is finite and determines the total distance.

# Task 03 – Proportionality

## Problem Statement

The gravitational force between two masses is

$F = G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2}$

Determine the factor by which the force changes if

- the distance $r$ is doubled  
- both masses are halved

---

## Theory

The gravitational force is proportional to

$$
F \propto \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2}
$$

Any change in masses or distance affects the force through this proportionality.

---

## Step-by-Step Solution

New values:

$$
m_1' = \frac{m_1}{2}
$$

$$
m_2' = \frac{m_2}{2}
$$

$$
r' = 2r
$$

Substitute into the formula:

$$
F' = G \frac{(m_1/2)(m_2/2)}{(2r)^2}
$$

$$
F' = G \frac{m_1 m_2}{4 \cdot 4 r^2}
$$

$$
F' = \frac{1}{16} G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2}
$$

---

## Final Result

$$
F' = \frac{F}{16}
$$

---

## Interpretation

The gravitational force becomes sixteen times smaller due to the combined reduction of mass and increase in distance.

# Task 08 – Definite Integrals

## Problem Statement

Calculate the area under the curve

$f(x) = \sin x$

between $x = 0$ and $x = \pi$.

---

## Theory

The definite integral gives the signed area under a function.

---

## Step-by-Step Solution

Area:

$$
A = \int_0^\pi \sin x \, dx
$$

Antiderivative:

$$
\int \sin x\,dx = -\cos x
$$

Evaluate the limits:

$$
A = [-\cos x]_0^\pi
$$

$$
A = -\cos(\pi) + \cos(0)
$$

$$
A = 1 + 1
$$

$$
A = 2
$$

---

## Final Result

$$
A = 2
$$

---

## Interpretation

The positive area under one half-period of the sine function equals two square units.

# Task 05 – Trigonometry

## Problem Statement

A vector has magnitude $15$ and forms an angle of $60^\circ$ with the horizontal axis.

Determine its horizontal and vertical components.

---

## Theory

For a vector with magnitude $A$ and angle $\theta$:

$$
A_x = A\cos\theta
$$

$$
A_y = A\sin\theta
$$

---

## Step-by-Step Solution

Horizontal component:

$$
A_x = 15\cos 60^\circ
$$

$$
A_x = 15 \cdot \frac{1}{2}
$$

$$
A_x = 7.5
$$

Vertical component:

$$
A_y = 15\sin 60^\circ
$$

$$
A_y = 15 \cdot \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}
$$

$$
A_y = \frac{15\sqrt{3}}{2}
$$

---

## Final Result

$$
A_x = 7.5
$$

$$
A_y = \frac{15\sqrt{3}}{2}
$$

---

## Interpretation

The vector can be represented as the sum of two perpendicular components along the horizontal and vertical axes.

# Task 06 – Function Analysis

## Problem Statement

Analyze the function

$f(x) = 3x^2 - 12x + 7$

and determine any local extrema.

---

## Theory

Local extrema occur at points where the first derivative equals zero.

The second derivative determines the nature of the extremum.

---

## Step-by-Step Solution

First derivative:

$$
f'(x) = 6x - 12
$$

Set derivative equal to zero:

$$
6x - 12 = 0
$$

$$
x = 2
$$

Second derivative:

$$
f''(x) = 6
$$

Since

$$
f''(x) > 0
$$

the point is a minimum.

Evaluate the function:

$$
f(2) = 3(2)^2 - 12(2) + 7
$$

$$
f(2) = 12 - 24 + 7
$$

$$
f(2) = -5
$$

---

## Final Result

Local minimum:

$$
(2,-5)
$$

---

## Interpretation

The parabola opens upward and reaches its lowest point at $x = 2$.

# Task 08 – EM Spectrum

## Problem Statement

List the following types of electromagnetic radiation in order of increasing wavelength: Infrared, Ultraviolet, Microwaves, X-rays, Radio waves, Gamma rays.

## Theory

The Electromagnetic Spectrum is categorized by frequency ($f$) and wavelength ($\lambda$). There is an inverse relationship between the two, governed by the speed of light $c$:

$$
c = \lambda f
$$

Higher energy waves have higher frequencies and shorter wavelengths, while lower energy waves have lower frequencies and longer wavelengths.

## Step-by-Step Solution

Ordering from shortest wavelength (highest frequency) to longest wavelength (lowest frequency), the approximate numerical ranges in a vacuum are:

### 1. Gamma rays
- **Wavelength:** $< 10^{-11} \text{ m}$
- **Frequency:** $> 30 \text{ EHz}$ ($3 \times 10^{19} \text{ Hz}$)

### 2. X-rays
- **Wavelength:** $0.01 \text{ nm}$ to $10 \text{ nm}$ ($10^{-11} \text{ m}$ to $10^{-8} \text{ m}$)
- **Frequency:** $30 \text{ PHz}$ to $30 \text{ EHz}$ ($3 \times 10^{16} \text{ Hz}$ to $3 \times 10^{19} \text{ Hz}$)

### 3. Ultraviolet
- **Wavelength:** $10 \text{ nm}$ to $400 \text{ nm}$ ($10^{-8} \text{ m}$ to $4 \times 10^{-7} \text{ m}$)
- **Frequency:** $7.5 \times 10^{14} \text{ Hz}$ to $3 \times 10^{16} \text{ Hz}$

### 4. Infrared
- **Wavelength:** $700 \text{ nm}$ to $1 \text{ mm}$ ($7 \times 10^{-7} \text{ m}$ to $10^{-3} \text{ m}$)
- **Frequency:** $300 \text{ GHz}$ to $4.3 \times 10^{14} \text{ Hz}$

### 5. Microwaves
- **Wavelength:** $1 \text{ mm}$ to $1 \text{ m}$ ($10^{-3} \text{ m}$ to $1 \text{ m}$)
- **Frequency:** $300 \text{ MHz}$ to $300 \text{ GHz}$ ($3 \times 10^{8} \text{ Hz}$ to $3 \times 10^{11} \text{ Hz}$)

### 6. Radio waves
- **Wavelength:** $1 \text{ m}$ to $100 \text{ km}$ ($1 \text{ m}$ to $10^5 \text{ m}$)
- **Frequency:** $3 \text{ kHz}$ to $300 \text{ MHz}$ ($3 \times 10^{3} \text{ Hz}$ to $3 \times 10^{8} \text{ Hz}$)



## Final Result

The order of increasing wavelength is:

**Gamma rays < X-rays < Ultraviolet < Infrared < Microwaves < Radio waves**

## Interpretation

As we move from Gamma rays to Radio waves, the wavelength increases by many orders of magnitude. For a presentation, it is useful to note that while Gamma rays are smaller than an atom's nucleus, Radio waves can be longer than a football field or even a mountain range.

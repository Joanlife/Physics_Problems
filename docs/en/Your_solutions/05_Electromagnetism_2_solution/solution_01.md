# Task 01 – Gauss's Law

## Problem Statement

A point charge of $q = +2 \text{ C}$ is located at the origin. Calculate the electric flux $\Phi_E$ through a spherical surface of radius $r = 1 \text{ m}$ centered at the origin.

## Theory

Gauss's Law relates the net electric flux through a closed surface (often called a Gaussian surface) to the net enclosed electric charge. The law is expressed as:

$$
\Phi_E = \frac{q_{enc}}{\epsilon_0}
$$

Where:
- $\Phi_E$ is the electric flux.
- $q_{enc}$ is the total charge enclosed by the surface.
- $\epsilon_0$ is the vacuum permittivity, approximately $8.854 \times 10^{-12} \text{ F/m}$.

Crucially, for a closed surface, the flux depends only on the enclosed charge and not on the radius or shape of the surface, provided the charge is inside.

## Step-by-Step Solution

### 1. Identify the Enclosed Charge
The point charge is located at the origin, and the spherical surface is centered at the origin with a radius of $1 \text{ m}$. Since the radius is finite and the charge is at the center, the entire charge $q$ is enclosed.

$$
q_{enc} = 2 \text{ C}
$$

### 2. Apply Gauss's Law
Since we need to find the total flux $\Phi_E$, we use the direct relationship:

$$
\Phi_E = \frac{q_{enc}}{\epsilon_0}
$$

### 3. Numerical Calculation
Substitute the known values:

$$
\Phi_E = \frac{2}{8.854 \times 10^{-12}}
$$

$$
\Phi_E \approx 2.259 \times 10^{11} \text{ V} \cdot \text{m}
$$

## Final Result

The electric flux through the spherical surface is:

$$
\Phi_E \approx 2.26 \times 10^{11} \text{ N} \cdot \text{m}^2 / \text{C}
$$

## Interpretation

The positive value of the flux indicates that the electric field lines are pointing outward from the surface, which is expected for a positive point charge. Note that the radius of $1 \text{ m}$ does not affect the final result; as long as the sphere encloses the charge, the total number of field lines passing through the surface remains constant.

# Task 07 – Wavelength and Frequency

## Problem Statement

The human eye is sensitive to light with a wavelength $\lambda = 550 \text{ nm}$.What color does this correspond to in the visible spectrum?  calculate the frequency $f$.

## Theory

The relationship between speed, wavelength, and frequency is:

$$
c = \lambda f \implies f = \frac{c}{\lambda}
$$

Where $c \approx 3 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$.

## Step-by-Step Solution

### 1. Color Identification
The visible spectrum ranges from roughly $400 \text{ nm}$ (violet) to $700 \text{ nm}$ (red). A wavelength of $550 \text{ nm}$ falls squarely in the **Green/Yellow-Green** portion of the spectrum.

### 2. Frequency Calculation
Convert $\lambda$ to meters: $550 \text{ nm} = 550 \times 10^{-9} \text{ m}$.

$$
f = \frac{3 \times 10^8}{550 \times 10^{-9}}
$$

$$
f = \frac{3}{550} \times 10^{17} \approx 0.00545 \times 10^{17} = 5.45 \times 10^{14} \text{ Hz}
$$

## Final Result

- Color: **Green**
- Frequency: $5.45 \times 10^{14} \text{ Hz}$ (or $545 \text{ THz}$)

## Interpretation

Green light is in the middle of the visible spectrum. This is the frequency range at which our retinal cells (photoreceptors) are most efficient at converting photons into electrical signals.

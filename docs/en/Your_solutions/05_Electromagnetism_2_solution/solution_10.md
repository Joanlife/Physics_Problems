# Task 10 – Speed of Light in Media

## Problem Statement

What is the speed of light $v$ in a diamond, which has an index of refraction $n = 2.42$?

## Theory

The index of refraction $n$ of a material is the ratio of the speed of light in a vacuum $c$ to the speed of light in that material $v$:

$$
n = \frac{c}{v} \implies v = \frac{c}{n}
$$

Where $c \approx 3 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$.

## Step-by-Step Solution

### 1. Identify Variables
- $c = 3.00 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$
- $n = 2.42$

### 2. Calculate the Speed
$$
v = \frac{3 \times 10^8}{2.42}
$$

$$
v \approx 1.239 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}
$$

## Final Result

The speed of light in diamond is approximately:

$$
v \approx 1.24 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}
$$

## Interpretation

The speed of light in diamond is significantly slower than in a vacuum . This high index of refraction is what contributes to the "brilliance" and dispersion of light in diamonds, instead of light just passing straight through the bottom, the high 'index' causes the light to get trapped and bounce around inside. This internal bouncing is why diamonds look so bright and sparkly to our eyes."

# Task 05 – Energy Stored in a Capacitor

## Problem Statement

A parallel-plate capacitor has:
- Area $S = 0.02 \text{ m}^2$
- Distance $d = 5 \text{ mm} = 0.005 \text{ m}$
- Voltage $U = 500 \text{ V}$

Calculate Capacitance ($C$), Stored Energy ($W$), Electric Field ($E$), and Attractive Force ($F$).

## Theory

The following are fundamental formulas for parallel-plate capacitors:
1. $C = \frac{\epsilon_0 S}{d}$
2. $W = \frac{1}{2} C U^2$
3. $E = \frac{U}{d}$
4. $F = \frac{1}{2} Q E,  Q = CU , F=\frac{1}{2} C U E$

- $\epsilon_0$ is the vacuum permittivity, approximately $8.854 \times 10^{-12} \text{ F/m}$.

## Step-by-Step Solution

### 1. Capacitance ($C$)
$$
C = \frac{8.854 \times 10^{-12} \times 0.02}{0.005}
$$

$$
C = 8.854 \times 10^{-12} \times 4 = 35.416 \times 10^{-12} \text{ F}
$$

### 2. Stored Energy ($W$)
$$
W = \frac{1}{2} \times (35.416 \times 10^{-12}) \times (500)^2
$$

$$
W = 0.5 \times 35.416 \times 10^{-12} \times 250,000 = 4.427 \times 10^{-6} \text{ J}
$$

### 3. Electric Field Intensity ($E$)
$$
E = \frac{500}{0.005} = 100,000 \text{ V/m}
$$

### 4. Force of Attraction ($F$)
$$
F = \frac{1}{2} C U E = \frac{1}{2} \times (35.416 \times 10^{-12}) \times 500 \times 100,000
$$

$$
F = 0.5 \times 35.416 \times 10^{-12} \times 50,000,000 = 8.854 \times 10^{-4} \text{ N}
$$

## Final Result

1. $C \approx 35.4 \text{ pF}$
2. $W \approx 4.43 \text{ \ J}$
3. $E = 10^5 \text{ V/m}$
4. $F \approx 0.885 \text{ N}$

## Interpretation

The force of attraction exists because the plates carry opposite charges. Note that as the voltage increases, the energy stored also5 increases.

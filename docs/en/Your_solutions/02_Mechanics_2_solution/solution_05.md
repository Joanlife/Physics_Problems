# Task 05 – Inelastic Collision: Runner and Cart

## Problem Statement

A $70\text{ kg}$ runner moving at $3\text{ m/s}$ jumps onto a $140\text{ kg}$ stationary cart. 
1. What is the final speed of the cart with the runner? 
2. Is kinetic energy conserved in this collision? Explain.

## Theory

In any collision where objects stick together, it is a **perfectly inelastic collision**. Linear momentum is always conserved in the absence of external horizontal forces:

$$
P_{initial} = P_{final}
$$

Kinetic energy ($K = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$) is **not** conserved in inelastic collisions; some energy is transformed into internal energy, heat, or sound.

## Step-by-Step Solution

### 1. Final Speed

Let $m_r = 70\text{ kg}$, $v_r = 3\text{ m/s}$, and $m_c = 140\text{ kg}$.

$$
m_r v_r = (m_r + m_c) V_f
$$

$$
70 \cdot 3 = (70 + 140) V_f
$$

$$
210 = 210 \cdot V_f \implies V_f = 1\text{ m/s}
$$

### 2. Kinetic Energy Analysis

Initial Kinetic Energy ($K_i$):

$$
K_i = \frac{1}{2} m_r v_r^2 = \frac{1}{2} \cdot 70 \cdot 3^2 = 35 \cdot 9 = 315\text{ J}
$$

Final Kinetic Energy ($K_f$):

$$
K_f = \frac{1}{2} (m_r + m_c) V_f^2 = \frac{1}{2} \cdot 210 \cdot 1^2 = 105\text{ J}
$$

## Final Result

1. The final speed is $1\text{ m/s}$.
2. Kinetic energy is **not** conserved ($105\text{ J} < 315\text{ J}$).

## Interpretation

Two-thirds of the initial kinetic energy ($210\text{ J}$) was lost during the jump. This energy was dissipated through the work done by friction between the runner's shoes and the cart to bring them to a common velocity, as well as biological damping in the runner's legs.

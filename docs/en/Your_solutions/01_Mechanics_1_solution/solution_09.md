# Task 09 – Momentum Comparison

## Problem Statement
Which has greater momentum:
1. A 2-gram fly flying at $10 \text{ m/s}$.
2. A 60-gram tennis ball moving at $1 \text{ m/s}$.

## Theory
Momentum $p$ is a vector quantity defined as the product of mass $m$ and velocity $v$:

$$
p = mv
$$

Mass must be in kilograms (kg) for standard SI units ($kg \cdot m/s$).

## Step-by-Step Solution

### 1. Momentum of the Fly
* $m_1 = 2 \text{ g} = 0.002 \text{ kg}$
* $v_1 = 10 \text{ m/s}$

$$
p_1 = 0.002 \cdot 10 = 0.02 \text{ kg}\cdot\text{m/s}
$$

### 2. Momentum of the Tennis Ball
* $m_2 = 60 \text{ g} = 0.06 \text{ kg}$
* $v_2 = 1 \text{ m/s}$

$$
p_2 = 0.06 \cdot 1 = 0.06 \text{ kg}\cdot\text{m/s}
$$

## Final Result
The tennis ball ($0.06 \text{ kg}\cdot\text{m/s}$) has greater momentum than the fly ($0.02 \text{ kg}\cdot\text{m/s}$).

## Interpretation
Even though the fly is 10 times faster, the tennis ball is 30 times heavier, making its total momentum higher.

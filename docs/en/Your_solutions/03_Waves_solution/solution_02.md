# Task 02 – String Harmonics

## Problem Statement

A guitar string is $64 \text{ cm}$ long and has a fundamental frequency (one antinode) of $330 \text{ Hz}$. What is the speed of the wave on this string?

## Theory

For a string fixed at both ends, standing waves are formed. The fundamental frequency (the first harmonic) corresponds to the simplest mode of vibration where the length of the string $L$ contains exactly half of a wavelength.

The relationship between string length and wavelength for the $n$-th harmonic is:

$$
L = n \frac{\lambda_n}{2}
$$

For the fundamental frequency ($n=1$):

$$
L = \frac{\lambda_1}{2} \implies \lambda_1 = 2L
$$

The wave speed $v$ is related to frequency $f$ and wavelength $\lambda$ by:

$$
v = f \lambda
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

### 1. Identify Given Parameters
* Length $L = 64 \text{ cm} = 0.64 \text{ m}$
* Fundamental frequency $f_1 = 330 \text{ Hz}$
* Mode $n = 1$ (fundamental)

### 2. Determine Wavelength
Based on the boundary conditions for a string fixed at both ends:

$$
\lambda_1 = 2 \times 0.64 \text{ m} = 1.28 \text{ m}
$$

### 3. Calculate Wave Speed
Using the wave equation:

$$
v = f_1 \lambda_1
$$

$$
v = 330 \text{ Hz} \times 1.28 \text{ m}
$$

$$
v = 422.4 \text{ m/s}
$$

## Final Result

The speed of the wave on the guitar string is:

$$
v = 422.4 \text{ m/s}
$$

## Interpretation

This speed represents the velocity at which a disturbance (transverse wave) travels along the string. This velocity is determined by the physical properties of the string, specifically the tension $T$ and the linear mass density $\mu$, according to the formula $v = \sqrt{T/\mu}$.

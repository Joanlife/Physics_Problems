# Task 07 – Standing Wave Modes

## Problem Statement

A standing wave with four antinodes is produced on a string of length $L = 80 \text{ cm}$. What is the wavelength of this wave?

## Theory

In a standing wave on a string fixed at both ends, the number of antinodes corresponds to the harmonic number $n$.
* $n=1$ (1 antinode): Fundamental frequency.
* $n=2$ (2 antinodes): Second harmonic.
* $n=4$ (4 antinodes): Fourth harmonic.

The relationship between the length of the string $L$ and the wavelength $\lambda_n$ is:

$$
L = n \frac{\lambda_n}{2}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

### 1. Identify Parameters
* String length $L = 80 \text{ cm} = 0.8 \text{ m}$
* Number of antinodes $n = 4$

### 2. Apply the Standing Wave Formula
Substitute the values into the formula for $L$:

$$
0.8 \text{ m} = 4 \left( \frac{\lambda_4}{2} \right)
$$

### 3. Solve for $\lambda_4$
Simplify the expression:

$$
0.8 \text{ m} = 2\lambda_4
$$

$$
\lambda_4 = \frac{0.8 \text{ m}}{2}
$$

$$
\lambda_4 = 0.4 \text{ m}
$$

## Final Result

The wavelength of the standing wave is:

$$
\lambda = 0.4 \text{ m} \quad (40 \text{ cm})
$$

## Interpretation

When a string vibrates in its 4th harmonic, the string is divided into four "loops." Each loop represents half a wavelength ($\lambda/2$). Since four half-wavelengths fit into $80 \text{ cm}$, one full wavelength must be $40 \text{ cm}$.

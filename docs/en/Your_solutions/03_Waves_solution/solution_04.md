# Task 04 – Phase Difference

## Problem Statement

What is the phase difference in radians between two points on a wave that are separated by a distance of $\lambda/3$?

## Theory

The phase of a wave $\phi$ describes the  relative displacement between two waves that share the same frequency . For a traveling wave described by $y(x,t) = A \sin(kx - \omega t + \phi_0)$, the phase at any point is the argument of the sine function.

The phase difference $\Delta \phi$ between two points separated by a distance $\Delta x$ is proportional to the ratio of that distance to the total wavelength $\lambda$. Since one full wavelength ($1\lambda$) corresponds to a phase shift of $2\pi$ radians (one full circle), the relationship is:

$$
\Delta \phi = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda} \Delta x
$$

Alternatively, using the wave number $k = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda}$:

$$
\Delta \phi = k \Delta x
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

### 1. Identify Given Parameters
The distance between the two points is given as:

$$
\Delta x = \frac{\lambda}{3}
$$

### 2. Calculate Phase Difference
Substitute $\Delta x$ into the phase difference formula:

$$
\Delta \phi = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda} \left( \frac{\lambda}{3} \right)
$$

The wavelength $\lambda$ cancels out:

$$
\Delta \phi = \frac{2\pi}{3} \text{ radians}
$$

## Final Result

The phase difference between the two points is:

$$
\Delta \phi = \frac{2\pi}{3} \text{ rad} \approx 2.09 \text{ rad}
$$

## Interpretation

A phase difference of $2\pi/3$ corresponds to $120^\circ$. This means that if the first point is at a peak (maximum displacement), the second point is $1/3$ of a cycle behind it, moving toward the next equilibrium position.

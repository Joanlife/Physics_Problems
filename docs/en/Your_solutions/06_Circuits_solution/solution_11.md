# Task 11 – Power and Energy Dissipation

## Problem Statement

A voltage of $50\,\text{V}$ is applied across a $100\,\Omega$ resistor.
1. What is the power ($P$) dissipated by the resistor?
2. How much energy ($E$) is consumed in $5$ minutes?

## Theory

### Electrical Power
Power is the rate at which electrical energy is converted into another form (such as heat) by a resistor. When voltage ($V$) and resistance ($R$) are known, power is calculated using:

$$
P = \frac{V^2}{R}
$$


### Electrical Energy
Energy is the total work done or power consumed over a specific period of time. It is the product of power and time:

$$
E = P \cdot t
$$

For the result to be in Joules ($\text{J}$), time must be in seconds ($\text{s}$).

## Step-by-Step Solution

### 1. Calculate Power Dissipation
Substitute the given values into the power formula:

$$
\begin{align}
P &= \frac{(50\,\text{V})^2}{100\,\Omega} \\
  &= \frac{2500}{100} \\
  &= 25\,\text{W}
\end{align}
$$

### 2. Unit Conversion for Time
The duration is given in minutes. Convert to the SI unit (seconds):

$$
t = 5\,\text{min} \times 60\,\text{s/min} = 300\,\text{s}
$$

### 3. Calculate Energy Consumption
Using the power calculated in step 1:

$$
\begin{align}
E &= 25\,\text{W} \cdot 300\,\text{s} \\
  &= 7500\,\text{J}
\end{align}
$$



## Final Result

- **Power Dissipated:** $25\,\text{W}$
- **Energy Consumed:** $7500\,\text{J}$ 

## Interpretation
The resistor converts $25\,\text{Joules}$ of electrical energy into thermal energy every second. Over a five-minute interval, the cumulative heat energy released is $7,500\text{J}$. This calculation is vital for ensuring that a resistor's power rating is not exceeded, which would lead to overheating and component failure.

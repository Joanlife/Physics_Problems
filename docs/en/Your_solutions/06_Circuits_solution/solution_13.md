# Task 13 – Transformer Turns Ratio

## Problem Statement

A transformer is used to step down the voltage from $120\,\text{V}$ AC to $9.0\,\text{V}$ AC. If the primary coil has $N_p = 400$ turns, calculate the number of turns required for the secondary coil ($N_s$).

## Theory

The operation of a transformer is based on Faraday's Law of Induction. The ratio of the secondary voltage to the primary voltage is directly proportional to the ratio of the number of turns in the secondary coil to the number of turns in the primary coil. This is known as the **transformer turns ratio equation**:

$$
\frac{V_s}{V_p} = \frac{N_s}{N_p}
$$

Where:
- $V_p$ and $V_s$ are the primary and secondary voltages.
- $N_p$ and $N_s$ are the number of turns in the primary and secondary coils.

## Step-by-Step Solution

### 1. Identify Known Values
- Primary Voltage ($V_p$): $120\,\text{V}$
- Secondary Voltage ($V_s$): $9.0\,\text{V}$
- Primary Turns ($N_p$): $400$

### 2. Rearrange the Equation
To find the secondary turns ($N_s$), we rearrange the formula:

$$
N_s = N_p \cdot \left( \frac{V_s}{V_p} \right)
$$

### 3. Calculate the Number of Turns
Substitute the given values:

$$
\begin{align}
N_s &= 400 \cdot \left( \frac{9.0\,\text{V}}{120\,\text{V}} \right) \\
    &= 400 \cdot 0.075 \\
    &= 30
\end{align}
$$

## Final Result

The secondary coil must have **30 turns**.

## Interpretation
Since the secondary voltage is lower than the primary voltage, this is a **step-down transformer**. The turns ratio ( $3:40$) indicates that for every 40 turns on the primary side, there must be 3 turns on the secondary side to achieve the desired voltage reduction.

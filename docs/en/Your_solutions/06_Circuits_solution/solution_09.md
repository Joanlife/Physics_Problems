# Task 09 – Instantaneous Current

## Problem Statement

The charge $Q(t)$ flowing through a wire is defined by the function:

$$
Q(t) = 5t^2 + 5
$$

Calculate the current $I$ at time $t = 3\,\text{s}$.

## Theory

Current is defined as the rate of flow of charge over time. Mathematically, it is the first derivative of the charge function with respect to time:

$$
I(t) = \frac{dQ}{dt}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

### 1. Differentiate the Charge Function
Given $Q(t) = 5t^2 + 5$, we find the derivative:

$$
\begin{align}
I(t) &= \frac{d}{dt}(5t^2 + 5) \\
     &= 10t + 0 \\
     &= 10t
\end{align}
$$

### 2. Evaluate at $t = 3\,\text{s}$
Substitute the given time into the derived current equation:

$$
\begin{align}
I(3) &= 10(3) \\
     &= 30\,\text{A}
\end{align}
$$

## Final Result

$$
I = 30\,\text{A}
$$

## Interpretation
The current is not constant; it increases linearly with time. At the 3-second mark, the rate of charge flow is 30 Coulombs per second.

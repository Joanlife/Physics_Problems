# Task 07 – Capacitors in Parallel

## Problem Statement

Two capacitors, $C_1 = 4\,\mu\text{F}$ and $C_2 = 6\,\mu\text{F}$, are connected in parallel to a $10\,\text{V}$ battery. Calculate:
1. The total charge stored ($Q_{tot}$).
2. The total energy stored ($U_{tot}$).

## Theory

### Parallel Capacitance
When capacitors are in parallel, the total capacitance is the sum of individual capacitances:

$$
C_{eq} = C_1 + C_2
$$

### Charge and Energy Formulas
- **Charge:** $Q = C \cdot V$
- **Energy:** $U = \frac{1}{2} C V^2$

## Step-by-Step Solution

### 1. Total Capacitance
First, we find the equivalent capacitance:

$$
\begin{align}
C_{eq} &= 4\,\mu\text{F} + 6\,\mu\text{F} \\
       &= 10\,\mu\text{F} = 10 \times 10^{-6}\,\text{F}
\end{align}
$$

### 2. Total Charge
The charge stored by the equivalent capacitor (and thus the total charge drawn from the battery) is:

$$
\begin{align}
Q_{tot} &= C_{eq} \cdot V \\
        &= (10 \times 10^{-6}\,\text{F}) \cdot (10\,\text{V}) \\
        &= 100 \times 10^{-6}\,\text{C} = 100\,\mu\text{C}
\end{align}
$$

### 3. Total Energy
The energy stored in the system:

$$
\begin{align}
U_{tot} &= \frac{1}{2} C_{eq} V^2 \\
        &= \frac{1}{2} (10 \times 10^{-6}\,\text{F}) (10\,\text{V})^2 \\
        &= \frac{1}{2} (10^{-5}) (100) \\
        &= 5 \times 10^{-4}\,\text{J} 
\end{align}
$$

## Final Result

- **Total Charge:**  $5 \times 10^{-4}\,\text{J}$
- **Total Energy:** $0.5\,\text{mJ}$

## Interpretation
In parallel, capacitors combine their ability to store charge. The voltage is identical across both, meaning the larger capacitor stores more charge and more energy than the smaller one.

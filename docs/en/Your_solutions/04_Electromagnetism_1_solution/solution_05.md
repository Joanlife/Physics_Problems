# Task 05 – Electric Field Levitation

## Problem Statement

What electric field strength is required to make a proton levitate against Earth's gravity at the level of the Earth's surface? 

**Given:**
- $m_p \approx 1.67 \times 10^{-27} \text{ kg}$
- $e \approx 1.6 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}$
- $g \approx 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$

## Theory

For an object to "levitate," the net force acting on it must be zero. Two forces are acting on the proton:
1. **Gravitational Force ($F_g$):** Acting downward, $F_g = mg$.
2. **Electric Force ($F_e$):** Acting upward, $F_e = qE$.

For equilibrium:

$$
F_e = F_g \implies qE = mg
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

### 1. Set Up the Balance Equation
We need to find the Electric Field $E$:

$$
E = \frac{mg}{q}
$$

### 2. Substitute Known Values
Using the mass and charge of the proton:

$$
E = \frac{(1.67 \times 10^{-27} \text{ kg})(9.8 \text{ m/s}^2)}{1.6 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}}
$$

### 3. Calculate the Result

$$
E \approx \frac{1.6366 \times 10^{-26}}{1.6 \times 10^{-19}}
$$

$$
E \approx 1.02 \times 10^{-7} \text{ N/C}
$$

## Final Result

The required electric field strength is:

$$
E \approx 1.02 \times 10^{-7} \text{ V/m (or N/C)}
$$

## Interpretation

The required field is incredibly small. This demonstrates how much stronger the electric force is compared to gravity; a tiny electric field is sufficient to counteract the entire gravitational pull of the Earth on a subatomic particle. For a presentation, one could note that this field must point **upward** because the proton is positively charged and needs an upward force.

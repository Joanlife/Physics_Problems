# Task 10 – Lorentz Force acting on Wire

## Problem Statement

A straight wire $2.0$ m long carries a current of $10$ A. It is placed in a uniform magnetic field of $B = 0.5$ T. Calculate the magnetic force on the wire if the angle between the wire and the magnetic field is:
a) $90^\circ$
b) $45^\circ$
c) $0^\circ$

## Theory

The magnetic force on a current-carrying wire is given by:
$$
F = BIL \sin(\theta)
$$

Where:
- $B$ is the magnetic field strength (Tesla)
- $I$ is the current (Amperes)
- $L$ is the length of the wire (Meters)
- $\theta$ is the angle between the current direction and the magnetic field.

## Step-by-Step Solution

### 1. Base Calculation ($BIL$)
First, calculate the constant part of the equation:
$$
B \cdot I \cdot L = (0.5 \text{ T}) \cdot (10 \text{ A}) \cdot (2.0 \text{ m}) = 10 \text{ N}
$$

### 2. Case (a): $\theta = 90^\circ$
$$
\sin(90^\circ) = 1
$$
$$
F = 10 \cdot 1 = 10 \text{ N}
$$

### 3. Case (b): $\theta = 45^\circ$
$$
\sin(45^\circ) = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} \approx 0.707
$$
$$
F = 10 \cdot 0.707 = 7.07 \text{ N}
$$

### 4. Case (c): $\theta = 0^\circ$
$$
\sin(0^\circ) = 0
$$
$$
F = 10 \cdot 0 = 0 \text{ N}
$$

## Final Result

- **a) $90^\circ$:** $10 \text{ N}$
- **b) $45^\circ$:** $7.07 \text{ N}$
- **c) $0^\circ$:** $0 \text{ N}$

## Interpretation

The force is strongest when the wire is perpendicular to the field. If the wire is parallel to the field ($0^\circ$), no magnetic force is produced at all. This is a crucial concept for designing electric motors, where the goal is to keep the wire segments as perpendicular to the field as possible to maximize torque.

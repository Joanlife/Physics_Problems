# Task 08 – Lorentz Force Magnitude

## Problem Statement

A charged particle with charge $q = 2 \times 10^{-19}$ C and mass $m = 4 \times 10^{-27}$ kg enters a magnetic field of $B = 0.5$ T at a speed of $v = 10^6$ m/s perpendicular to the field. What is the magnitude of the Lorentz force acting on the particle?

## Theory

The **Lorentz Force** $\vec{F}$ on a point charge moving in a magnetic field is given by the vector cross product:
$$
\vec{F} = q(\vec{v} \times \vec{B})
$$

The magnitude of this force is calculated as:
$$
F = qvB \sin(\theta)
$$

Where $\theta$ is the angle between the velocity vector and the magnetic field vector. If the movement is **perpendicular**, then $\theta = 90^\circ$ and $\sin(90^\circ) = 1$, simplifying the formula to:
$$
F = qvB
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

### 1. Identify the Variables
- $q = 2 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}$
- $v = 10^6 \text{ m/s}$
- $B = 0.5 \text{ T}$
- $\theta = 90^\circ$

### 2. Calculate the Magnitude
Substitute the values directly into the simplified equation:
$$
F = (2 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}) \cdot (10^6 \text{ m/s}) \cdot (0.5 \text{ T})
$$

$$
F = (2 \cdot 10^6 \cdot 0.5) \times 10^{-19}
$$

$$
F = 1 \times 10^{-13} \text{ N}
$$

## Final Result

The magnitude of the Lorentz force is:
$$
F = 1.0 \times 10^{-13} \text{ N}
$$

## Interpretation

While $10^{-13}$ N sounds small, remember the particle's mass is only $10^{-27}$ kg. Using $F=ma$, the acceleration is roughly $2.5 \times 10^{13} \text{ m/s}^2$. This massive acceleration explains why magnetic fields can bend the paths of particles almost instantly.
